Stealing money, tacking advantage of an exploit in a system have been going on for a long time in many different domains: legal, financial, political, and social.

Most recently computers have been the storage and device that powers modern financial infrastructure and as new technologies focus more on utility, they succumb to security threats as was the case with the internet since the 90s. 

Nowadays a lot more activity has been going on in the crypto space as blockchains that secure millions and billions of dollars worth of assets are powered by new systems which standards and conventions for security have not been established yet.

Most notably the North Korean Lazarus group has been involved in hacking several blockchain bridges, purportedly stealing 2 billions dollars worth of assets and laundering them to avoid US imposed sanctions for the development of Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD). Today I am going to go over the techniques the Lazarus group used to hack the two crypto bridges which is where the latest battle for financial gains and cyberwarfare is occurring.


North Korea was involved in two bridges hacks.
Both employed two different methods and shook not only the crypto industry but the cyber defense and intelligence community around the world resulting in the US government issuing an official warning early this year.


Axie Infinity
Out of the 9 validators, 4 of them were managed by one company which was hacked by North Korea. And additionally one more validator managed by AxieDAO was hacked to make two fradulant transactions and siphon millions of dollars out of the bridge. The validators' private keys were leaked through social engineering where North Korean hackers sent a recruitment offer to an Axie employee on LinkedIn and embedded malware in an attached file. 

Wormhole
Exploit in the smart contract. One of the parameters of the function was supposed to verify the authenticity of the signer.
Since the attack occurred only hours after a commit to patch the exploit was made, before it was pushed, supposedly the Lazarus group has been looking through the commit history to find exploits more efficiently (which honestly is a great technique to hack open source software).

North Korea's cyberwarfare division.
State-sponsored cyber terrorism for financial gain in blockchain is unprecedented. They not only employ sophisticated technical attacks but also rely on social engineering and have the patience to set the trap and wait before completing the deal. The Lazarus Group operate together in a building where hackers are all trying to infiltrate different blockchains. They copied crypto developers' resumes and used fake identities. Here is a copy of one of the North Korean hackers' resume. They are heavily employing social engineering as well to infiltrate organizations and find exploits to the system. 

The end of mixers?
The biggest Ethereum based Mixer, Tonado Cash, has processed over 7 billion dollars worth of assets and according to a study, about 2 billion dollars were used for laundering. 

Effect on the future of crypto
Tornado Cash being banned in the US was shocking news. A lot of people have speculated that it was used for money laundering for a while but the recent North Korean attack was what the government referred to as the main reason for its decision. Crypto is supposed to be about decentralization, empowering individuals, and more freedom with financial assets. Intervention by the state in different forms (hacking or regulation) are against this ethos and is hindering the prospects for this industry. If crypto is going to go all the way, and deliver its full potential, replacing the dollar and central currencies, collisions with states are inevitable. I personally think this incident suggests that the co-existance of the two financial systems -- a decentralized crypto economy and centralized state sponsored economy -- is going to be very challenging. 


Are cross-chain and crypto bridges really the future?
It might seem obvious that there should exist methods to easily transfer assets between different blockchains, however there are major drawbacks and Vitalik Buterin is against this idea.
