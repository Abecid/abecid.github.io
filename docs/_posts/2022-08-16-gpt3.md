<h2>Brief Summary & Significance</h2>
This paper has demonstrated that with larger the parameters, Large Language Models (LLMs) perform better on benchmarks and it argues for a general language model trained by unsupervised learning in contrast to previously popular task-specific fine-tuned models trained through labeled data. It has displayed one/few/zero shot learning. With ever growing sizes of language models, this paper releases the results for several large language models called GPT-3 including one model with 175B parameters.

<h2>Zero/One/Few-Shot Learning</h2>
<!-- Fig2.1 -->
<img src="/assets/posts/paper_review/2.gpt3/4.fig2-1.png">
<h4>Fine-Tuning</h4>
    Updating the weights of a pre-trained model for a specific task with labeled data. GPT-3 is not fine-tuned.
<h4>Few-Shot Learning</h4>
    The model is provided with several examples at inference time for reference, but the weights are not updated.
<h4>One-Shot Learning</h4>
    The model is provided with one example at inference time for reference, but the weights are not updated.
<h4>Zero-Shot Learning</h4>
    The model is not provided with any examples at inference time. 

<h2>Model and Training</h2>
The model architecture is very similar to that of the previous language model, GPT-2, except for the use of "alternating dense and locally banded sparse attention patterns in the layers of the transformer".

<!-- Table 2.2 -->
<img src="/assets/posts/paper_review/2.gpt3/6.table2-2.png">
The Common Crawl dataset was used for the majority of the training data constituting 400B tokens. 

<!-- Table 2.1 -->
<img src="/assets/posts/paper_review/2.gpt3/5.table2-1.png">
To analyze the correlation between performance and model size, 8 different sizes of model were trained from 125M to 175B learnable parameters. All models use a context window of 2048 tokens which is twice as big as that of GPT-2.

<img src="/assets/posts/paper_review/2.gpt3/7.figure2-2.png">
To prevent running out of memory while training large models, the researchers used "a mixture of model parallelism within each matrix multiply and model parallelism across the layers of the network".

All models were trained on V100 GPUs.

Compared to the smallest model, GPT-3 Small, which has 125M parameters, the largest model, GPT-3 175 B, used around three orders of magnitude of computer (petaflop/s-days) to train.

<h2>Results</h2>
<!-- Fig1.2, 1.3, 3.4 -->
<img src="/assets/posts/paper_review/2.gpt3/2.fig1-2.png">
Larger models make increasingly efficient use of in-context information

<img src="/assets/posts/paper_review/2.gpt3/3.fig1-3.png">
"While zero-shot performance improves steadily with model size, few-shot performance increases more rapidly, demonstrating that larger models are more proficient at in-context learning."

<img src="/assets/posts/paper_review/2.gpt3/8.fig3-4.png">
As the model gets bigger, there is a consistent trend of improvement across all datasets and translation into English tend to be stronger than translation from English.
